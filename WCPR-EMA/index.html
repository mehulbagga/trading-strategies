Strategy Overview

Core Concept

The Weekly CPR + EMA Strategy combines the powerful weekly Central Pivot Range (CPR) with dual EMA trend confirmation to identify high-probability trend continuation trades in major, minor, and cross currency pairs.

The Weekly CPR acts as a dynamic support/resistance zone and a market sentiment gauge. The 21 and 50 EMAs define the short-to-medium term trend direction. Trades are taken only when price breaks clearly out of the CPR zone, EMAs confirm the trend, and the 200 EMA validates the macro bias — ensuring you trade with the dominant flow, not against it.

Understanding Weekly CPR

What is the Central Pivot Range?

The CPR is a three-level pivot zone calculated from the previous week's High, Low, and Close. Unlike a single pivot point, CPR gives a price zone that acts as a gravity area — markets are attracted to it and then react sharply when breaking out of it.

PP = (Prev Week High + Prev Week Low + Prev Week Close) / 3
BC = (Prev Week High + Prev Week Low) / 2
TC = (PP − BC) + PP
// CPR Band = BC (bottom) to TC (top), with PP as the midline

Narrow CPR (Trending Market)

  • TC and BC are very close together
  • Signals that the market has strong directional bias
  • Breakouts from narrow CPR tend to be explosive
  • Best setup for trend-following entries
  • Target: CPR width < 20 pips (major pairs)

Wide CPR (Ranging Market)

  • TC and BC are far apart
  • Signals indecision or consolidation ahead
  • Price is likely to chop within the CPR zone
  • Avoid trend-following entries — wait for CPR to narrow
  • Wide CPR = potential mean-reversion market

EMA Framework

EMA 21 — Fast Trend

  • Captures short-term momentum
  • Price above EMA 21 = near-term bullish
  • Acts as dynamic support in uptrends
  • Primary signal trigger line

EMA 50 — Medium Trend

  • Defines the medium-term trend direction
  • 21 EMA cross above/below 50 EMA = trend shift signal
  • Acts as stronger support/resistance
  • Entry confirmation layer

EMA 200 — Macro Filter

  • Defines the macro/institutional trend
  • Only take longs above 200 EMA
  • Only take shorts below 200 EMA
  • Keeps you aligned with smart money

EMA Stack Alignment (Key Filter)

▲ BULLISH STACK

🟢 EMA 21 > EMA 50 > EMA 200
🟢 Price > EMA 21 (or pullback to it)
🟢 Price above Weekly CPR (TC)

▼ BEARISH STACK

🔴 EMA 21 < EMA 50 < EMA 200
🔴 Price < EMA 21 (or pullback to it)
🔴 Price below Weekly CPR (BC)
Entry Rules

Long Entry — Step by Step

1

Macro Filter

Confirm price is above the 200 EMA. This keeps trades aligned with the dominant institutional trend.

2

CPR Position Check

Price must be above the Weekly TC. The CPR zone is now support. If price is inside or below CPR, skip the trade.

3

EMA Trend Confirmation

EMA 21 must be above EMA 50. The EMA cross (21 crossing above 50) is the trigger signal — enter on candle close after the cross.

4

Distance Check

Price must be at least 5+ pips away from CPR TC to avoid false breakouts.

5

Execute

Enter long. Set Stop Loss 1.5× ATR below entry and Take Profit 3× ATR above entry. Enable trailing stop at 2× ATR.

Short Entry — Step by Step

1

Macro Filter

Confirm price is below the 200 EMA. Institutional bias is bearish.

2

CPR Position Check

Price must be below the Weekly BC. CPR is now overhead resistance — sellers are in control.

3

EMA Trend Confirmation

EMA 21 must be below EMA 50. Trigger: EMA 21 crossing below EMA 50.

4

Distance Check

Price must be at least 5+ pips below CPR BC to validate the breakdown.

5

Execute

Enter short. SL 1.5× ATR above entry, TP 3× ATR below entry. Trail at 2× ATR.

Stop Loss Placement Options

  • ATR-Based (Default): 1.5× ATR. Adapts to current volatility. Best for all conditions.
  • CPR-Based: Stop below BC (longs) or above TC (shorts). More context-aware.
  • Structure-Based: Below last swing low (longs) / above swing high (shorts). Best on 4H.
  • Trailing Stop: Move to break-even after 1R profit, trail at 2× ATR thereafter.

Timeframe-Specific Rules

1H Timeframe Rules

  • More signals, more active management required
  • Use during London & New York session overlap (12:00–16:00 GMT)
  • Avoid Asian session for EUR/GBP pairs
  • ATR-based SL is crucial — 1H is noisier
  • EMA cross on 1H must align with 4H trend

4H Timeframe Rules

  • Fewer but higher-quality signals
  • Best for set-and-forget with trailing stops
  • Wider SL needed — use 2.0× ATR multiplier
  • Align with Daily chart trend for maximum edge
  • Weekly CPR levels are extremely effective on 4H

Multi-Timeframe Confluence (Highest Probability Setups)

The strongest setups occur when both 1H and 4H timeframes align:

⚡ 4H trend direction matches the 1H EMA cross signal
⚡ Weekly CPR level on 4H is respected as support/resistance
⚡ 1H EMA 21 crosses above/below 50 EMA in direction of 4H trend
⚡ Daily close above/below 200 EMA — macro confirmation
⚡ Narrow CPR on current week = explosive trend likely

Filters — Avoid These Conditions

Do NOT Trade When:

🚫 Price is inside the CPR band — no clear directional bias
🚫 Wide CPR (>30+ pips on major pairs) — choppy week ahead
🚫 EMA 21 and EMA 50 are flat/horizontal — no momentum
🚫 Major news events within next 2 hours (NFP, CPI, FOMC, BOE, ECB)
🚫 Price at a major round number (e.g., 1.2000, 150.00) — high rejection risk
🚫 Friday after 18:00 GMT — low volume, weekend gap risk
🚫 EMA cross signal against the weekly/daily trend — countertrend signal
🚫 Spread is 3× wider than normal — avoid during thin liquidity

// ── Weekly CPR Calculation ───────────────────────────────── w_high = request.security(syminfo.tickerid, "W", high[1], lookahead=barmerge.lookahead_on) w_low = request.security(syminfo.tickerid, "W", low[1], lookahead=barmerge.lookahead_on) w_close = request.security(syminfo.tickerid, "W", close[1], lookahead=barmerge.lookahead_on) weekly_pp = (w_high + w_low + w_close) / 3 // Pivot Point weekly_bc = (w_high + w_low) / 2 // Bottom Central weekly_tc = (weekly_pp - weekly_bc) + weekly_pp // Top Central // ── EMA Stack ────────────────────────────────────────────── ema21 = ta.ema(close, 21) ema50 = ta.ema(close, 50) ema200 = ta.ema(close, 200) bull_trend = ema21 > ema50 and ema50 > ema200 bear_trend = ema21 < ema50 and ema50 < ema200 // ── Entry Conditions ─────────────────────────────────────── long_condition = ta.crossover(ema21, ema50) // EMA 21 crosses above 50 and close > weekly_tc // Price above CPR and close > ema200 // Above 200 EMA (macro bull) short_condition = ta.crossunder(ema21, ema50) // EMA 21 crosses below 50 and close < weekly_bc // Price below CPR and close < ema200 // Below 200 EMA (macro bear) // ── ATR Risk Management ──────────────────────────────────── atr = ta.atr(14) long_sl = close - atr * 1.5 // 1.5× ATR stop long_tp = close + atr * 3.0 // 3.0× ATR target (2:1 R:R)
